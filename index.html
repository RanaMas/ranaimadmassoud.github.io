Biography

I am a computational scientist with a PhD in physical chemistry specializing in molecular dynamics and computational modeling of chemical–biological interactions.

My current research focuses on computational toxicology, with an emphasis on environmental contaminants, PFAS, and mechanistic understanding of chemical toxicity in biological systems.

I am particularly interested in bridging molecular simulation and toxicological risk assessment to support evidence-based decisions in chemical safety and environmental health.

Research Statement

My research aims to integrate computational chemistry, molecular modeling, and toxicology to understand how environmental chemicals interact with biological systems and contribute to adverse outcomes.

I focus on translating molecular-level insights into meaningful frameworks for chemical risk assessment, prioritization, and regulatory decision making.
