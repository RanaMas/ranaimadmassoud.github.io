Biography

I am a computational scientist with a PhD in physical chemistry specializing in molecular dynamics, enhanced sampling, pharmacophore and computational modeling of small molecule-protein interactions.

My current research focuses on computational toxicology, with an emphasis on environmental contaminants, PFAS, and mechanistic understanding of chemical toxicity in biological systems. In addition, I am part of several computational drug design projects targeting tuberculosis and cancer.

I am particularly interested in bridging molecular simulation and toxicological risk assessment to support evidence-based decisions in chemical safety, policy, environmental and public human health. My projects support the new approach methods (NAMs).

Research Statement

My research aims to integrate computational chemistry, molecular modeling, and toxicology to understand how environmental chemicals interact with biological systems and contribute to adverse outcomes.

I focus on translating molecular-level insights into meaningful frameworks for chemical risk assessment, prioritization, and regulatory decision making.
